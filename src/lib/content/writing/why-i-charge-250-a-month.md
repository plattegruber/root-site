The cheapest way to get a dental website is to sign up with one of the
big template platforms. You’ll pay $40 a month and get something that
looks like every other dentist on it. The expensive way is to hire an
agency. You’ll pay $8,000 upfront and $400 a month and get something
that looks like every other agency client.

I’m between those two, deliberately. Here’s the math.

A custom-designed site takes me about two weeks. Maybe three. I have to
make a living, so I price the work to support that. But I also know
that a website isn’t a one-time purchase. The web changes. Your
practice changes. Things break, things need updates, you’ll want to
add a page, you’ll want to change a photo. Charging once and walking
away means you eventually have a site that’s frozen in 2024.

So I built the price around the long arrangement instead of the one-
time build. $250 a month, no setup fee. You get the design work, the
hosting, the speed, the security patches, the certificate renewals, and
me — answering an email when you need something changed. There’s no
contract. You can leave whenever.

At $250, I can keep my client list small enough that I know everyone
by name. I can answer your email the same day. I can build the site
the way I’d build my own. The number isn’t arbitrary. It’s the price
that lets me do the work I want to do, the way I want to do it.

If that’s the deal you want, get in touch.
