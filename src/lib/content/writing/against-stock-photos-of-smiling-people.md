---
title: The case against stock photos of smiling people
date: 2026-03-10
excerpt: A wall of beaming faces in scrubs doesn’t make anyone feel at ease. Here’s what to do instead.
---

Look at any dental website built by an agency in the last decade. Scroll
to the section called “Our Team” or “Why Choose Us.” You’ll see the same
photos. The young woman in scrubs holding a clipboard, head tilted at
exactly fifteen degrees. The older couple grinning while a dentist
hovers in the background. The toddler getting a high-five.

Nobody is fooled. You can tell stock photos from a hundred feet away.
And the moment a patient realizes the people on your website aren’t
real, every other claim on the page starts to feel a little less real
too.

The reason agencies use them is simple: they’re cheap, they’re fast, and
the dentist almost never argues. The result is that thousands of dental
practices are using the same handful of photographs, taken in the same
fake exam rooms, by photographers who have never met a patient.

Here’s what I tell my clients. Hire a local photographer for one
afternoon. Have them take photos of:

- The actual front of your building, from across the street.
- The waiting room when it’s empty. The chair you sit in.
- The exam rooms — pulled back, with light coming in. Not close-up.
- You and your team, doing your jobs. Not posed.

A photographer who knows what they’re doing will charge between $800
and $1,500 for this. You will use those photos for ten years. They will
do more for your website than any amount of copywriting.

The photos don’t need to be artful. They need to be real. That’s the
whole difference.
